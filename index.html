Buy now, or wait for your price?

Two buttons on every exchange. Market order fills instantly but pays a fee. Limit order is free but might never fill if the price runs away. Answer two questions:

1 · How jumpy is this market today?
2 · Chance it runs off before your limit fills?

About the "rewards" popups: those pay market-makers to park orders all day. At personal size they earn pennies while pros take the pool — and one bad fill costs days of rewards. Use limit orders to skip the fee, not to farm rewards.

How this all works, in 60 seconds

1 · Price = probability. A "Yes" share costing 14¢ pays $1 if the thing happens. So the market thinks: 14% chance.

2 · You can bet no. Betting something WON'T happen costs (100 − price)¢ and pays $1 if it doesn't.

3 · You can sell anytime. No need to wait years — sell whenever the price has moved your way.

4 · Two stores sell these. Kalshi (US-regulated, your payout is guaranteed by a clearinghouse, cash held at big banks) and Polymarket (cheaper, but crypto-based — you trust code and your own wallet). Neither is FDIC insured.

5 · Taxes exist. Profits are taxable. There's no IRS rule for these yet; most defensible route is capital-gains treatment with clean records — use "download my bets" on the My bets tab and talk to a CPA. Not tax advice.

6 · The golden rule. Every bet here rises and falls on one person's fortunes. Keep the whole theme under ~5% of your money (set yours in ⚙).
