03Map versus marketthe tradeable number
What this page produces: a probability that the Democratic nominee — whoever the Polling Desk says that is likely to be — wins the White House, given a national mood you choose.
How to trade it: Polymarket and Kalshi price "Democrat wins the presidency 2028" every day. If your simulated probability sits well above the market price at a mood you actually believe, that is a bet; well below, the other side is. The Ossoff desk handles fees, venues, and sizing from there.
The honest caveat: the map's default is just 2024 plus your slider. It knows nothing about candidates, scandals, or the economy in 2028. It is a machine for turning your opinion about the national mood into an electoral-vote probability — not a crystal ball.
04Methodologywhat the machine does
Baseline. Each state starts at its 2024 presidential margin. Maine and Nebraska are treated statewide (their district splits are worth 2 EV of precision we trade for clarity).
Each simulation draws one national error (how wrong the mood is, everywhere at once) and one per-state error (local surprises), both bell curves set by your uncertainty slider. A state's simulated margin = 2024 margin + your mood shift + both errors. Win the state, take its electoral votes; 270 wins the election. We run 20,000 of these per slider move.
Correlated error is the whole game. The national draw moves all states together — that is why the histogram has fat tails and why 269–269 ties appear. Models that miss this (most amateur ones) are overconfident. Ours inherits the lesson 538 taught everyone in 2016.
Locks. Clicking a state overrides the simulation for it — you are saying "I know this one." The odds recompute around your certainty.
