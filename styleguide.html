02Typethree voices, three jobs
Georgia serif — headlines, bet names, big numbers. The editorial voice.
Helvetica sans — body copy, explanations. The plain-English voice.
Mono caps — labels, tickers, kickers, method notes. The machine voice.
03Chartsnever PowerPoint
Direct labels, not legends-as-decoration. Series end with a dot and the current value at the line's right end, in the series color.
Everything interactive sits below the chart. Legend (clickable, click a name to hide it), then range buttons 1W / 1M / 3M / 1Y (3M default), then view modes. Nothing above the plot but data.
Hover everywhere. Crosshair, dots on every visible series, tooltip with date and values.
Curves, not kinks. Series are sampled weekly and drawn as smoothed beziers — never straight month-to-month polylines. Plots are open: no frame, no card border, three hairline gridlines, and the axis starts near the data instead of zero unless zero means something.
Quiet grid. Half-pixel rules at low opacity; axis numbers in mono; no chart junk, no drop shadows.
Honest data labels. LIVE means fetched this minute; anything else says SAVED COPY, NOT LIVE. Placeholders announce themselves.
04Article structurethe template, in words
Open with the best interactive visual. Section 01 is always something to touch, never a wall of text. Every section after it gets a visual, a table, or fact cards to break the text.
Numbered section banners. Double rule, ember number, serif title, mono dek on the right. Tabs (when present) scroll with a sticky-header offset and highlight on scroll.
The money chart goes high. Whatever the reader came for sits in section 01 or 02, never below the fold of methodology.
Boxed rows are lists, not essays. Bold serif lead-in, at most two sentences. Longer than that, it becomes prose or its own section.
Full-width prose. Leads run the column; no orphan half-width paragraphs floating beside empty space.
Verdict colors. BUY and gains are green (go); WAIT and losses are ember (hold). Bet names are bold serif, never colored.
Jargon pays a toll. Any technical term gets a plain-English definition in the same sentence, the first time, every article. Units are always explicit — a percent says a percent of what.
Downloads are cards. Per-article relevant PDFs only, whole card clickable, file type and size on a chip, raw working files kept in the repo.
Every claim computed, never hardcoded. If a number can come from live data, it does; if it cannot, it is labeled as a snapshot.
05Controlsselects, buttons, chips
Buttons are rectangular, mono caps, hairline border; the active one turns ember (border and text). Arrow pagers ‹ › flank a serif title when cycling views — never a second row of identical buttons.
Dropdowns use the dark card background with the sans face; one per decision, with the safe default preselected and each option written as advice, not just a value ("5% — typical"). Native menus render with the browser's own colors — acceptable, never fought with hacks.
Legend chips are clickable buttons with a ✓ or ✕, a color swatch, and a border — never bare text. Off state: dashed border, struck through, dimmed.
Inputs are wide enough for their number, right-aligned, serif digits, gold-free: focus rings are ember.
06The front pagenewspaper rules
Order: badge mark, masthead, crawl ticker, lead story, secondary row, what-every-desk-includes, library, footer.
The crawl runs directly under the masthead: one line, mono, all live markets (both parties' fields, the presidency, the desk's own legs, poll averages, the house call), duplicated content scrolling right-to-left, paused on hover, venue always named.
The lead story is a two-column grid: serif headline and dek on the left, a live interactive chart on the right with hover crosshair — the page's best visual, never a stock image.
Secondary stories sit in a bordered row beneath: each opens with a small data glyph (mini bars, tile map), then a mono kicker (LIVE green, IN RESEARCH gray), serif headline, two-line dek. Side placement on wide screens, stacking underneath on small ones.
Every page links back here. The footer of every desk names this guide; new pages start from template.html and follow it, no exceptions.
